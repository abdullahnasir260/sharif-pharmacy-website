Surviving the Punjab Winter: A Guide to Dry Skin Relief

Winter in Kotla Arab Ali Khan can be quite harsh. The dry winds combined with low humidity levels can quickly strip the natural oils from your skin, leading to itching, cracking, and discomfort.

Switch from Lotions to Creams

Most thin lotions are water-based. In the cold, that water evaporates, leaving your skin dry again within minutes. We recommend using thick creams or ointments. Products containing Petroleum Jelly or Glycerin work best to lock in moisture.

"Pharmacist Tip: The best time to moisturize is exactly 3 minutes after your shower. Pat your skin dry with a towel, but keep it slightly damp before applying your cream."

Don't Forget the Sunscreen

Just because it's cold doesn't mean the sun isn't damaging your skin. UV rays are still active in winter. If you are spending time outdoors, apply a basic SPF 30 sunscreen to prevent winter sunburn and premature aging.
