The Science of Supplements: Are You Taking Them Correctly?

Buying vitamins is an investment in your health. However, many people take them at the wrong time, meaning their body only absorbs a small fraction of the nutrients.

Morning vs. Evening

  • Morning: Vitamin C and B-Complex. These provide energy and can interfere with sleep if taken at night.
  • Evening: Magnesium. It helps relax your muscles and can improve your sleep quality.
Pharmacist's Warning: Vitamin D3 and Vitamin E are fat-soluble. They MUST be taken with a meal that contains some fat (like breakfast or dinner). Taking them on an empty stomach is almost useless.

Avoid the Tea Trap

In Pakistan, we love our tea. But drinking tea or coffee within 1 hour of taking your vitamins (especially Iron or Multivitamins) can block absorption. Use plain water instead.
