Cardiology Secrets for a Healthy Heart

Protecting the hearts of Kotla Arab Ali Khan

Heart disease remains one of the leading health concerns in our community. While genetics play a role, Cardiology experts agree that 80% of heart issues can be prevented through simple lifestyle changes and early detection.

Know Your Numbers

Regularly monitor your Blood Pressure. A consistent reading above 140/90 can silently damage your arteries over time.

The 30-Minute Rule

Brisk walking for just 30 minutes a day can lower your risk of heart attack by up to 40%. Consistency is the secret.

Reduce Salt & Oil

Excessive salt raises blood pressure, while reused cooking oils increase bad cholesterol. Opt for fresh, homemade meals.

Quality Sleep

Sleep deprivation triggers stress hormones that put a heavy load on your heart. Aim for 7-8 hours of restful sleep.

Red Flags You Should Never Ignore

Our pharmacists often consult with patients who ignore early signs. If you experience any of the following, consult a doctor immediately:

  • Unusual shortness of breath during light activity.
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the center of the chest.
  • Swelling in the feet, ankles, or legs (Edema).
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness when standing up.
